MISCONCEPTIONS

Islam is synonymous with violence and terrorism

The term "terrorism" and "violence" are immediately perceived and associated under Islamic and Muslim acts. It is a grossly biased, distorted and, above all, perfectly inaccurate perception. Terrorism does not define any kind of religion.

ISIS members are Muslims

ISIS is a militant group aiming to implement their own political view to the world through extremism. Though most members are known to be Arabs, not all are considered and are Muslims, because the Islamic State of Iraq and Syria (ISIS) believes and follows Jihadism, which is defined as a military movement “rooted in Islam,” nut not necessarily Islam, because Islam is a religion of peace and does not support any violence, whatsoever.

Muslims do not share good and noble values with non-Muslims

Like any other religion, they too believe in a god whom they call Allah, who they believe as their one and only God, like the Catholics who believe in God the father. Muslims also pray, five time a day to be exact, to profess themselves to Allah. Islamic beliefs about the afterlife is also important as well as them being a religion of peace.
